From 3 weeks to 2 days: a transparency agent for governance reporting

8 min read · Higher education · Illustrative engagement

The challenge. A mid-sized university spent nearly three weeks every quarter assembling governance reports — pulling numbers from the student information system, finance, HR and the learning platform into spreadsheets, then reconciling them by hand for council meetings and accreditation bodies. The process was slow, error-prone, and worst of all, opaque: when a trustee asked "where did this number come from?", no one could answer quickly.

What we built

Agentific designed a transparency & reporting agent that connects (read-only) to the institution's core systems and assembles governance reports on demand. Crucially, it was built around one principle: every figure must trace back to its source.

  • Plain-language reports. Leaders ask, "Summarise enrolment trends and fee collection for this quarter," and get a clear written brief with charts.
  • Click-through provenance. Every number links to the exact query and source record behind it — so any claim can be verified in seconds.
  • Accreditation templates. Pre-mapped to NAAC and NBA reporting structures, so staff stop reformatting the same data for each body.
  • Human sign-off. Nothing is published until a named officer reviews and approves — the agent drafts, people decide.

How transparency was guaranteed

We deployed the agent inside the university's own environment. It has read-only access, logs every query it runs, and produces an audit trail that shows what was asked, what data was used, and who approved the output. There are no black boxes: a reviewer can always see the chain from headline number to raw record.

"For the first time, when council asks where a figure comes from, we can show them — live, in the meeting." — Registrar (illustrative)

The results

  • Report preparation fell from ~3 weeks to ~2 days.
  • Reconciliation errors dropped sharply because numbers came straight from source, not re-keyed spreadsheets.
  • Audit queries resolved in minutes, not days, thanks to click-through provenance.
  • Staff time was redirected from copy-paste to analysis and decision-making.

Why it matters

Transparency isn't a feature you bolt on — it's a design choice. By making provenance the default, the institution got faster reporting and stronger accountability at the same time. That combination is exactly what regulators, trustees and the public increasingly expect.
