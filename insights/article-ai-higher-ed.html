5 places to start with AI in higher education

6 min read · For vice-chancellors, registrars & deans

AI is everywhere in the headlines and nowhere in most institutions' day-to-day operations. The gap isn't ambition — it's knowing where to start so you get a real win without betting the institution on it. Here are five proven starting points, roughly in order of speed-to-value.

1. A 24/7 student support agent

Students ask the same few hundred questions — fees, deadlines, hostel, timetables, scholarships — over and over. An AI agent grounded in your own handbook and policies answers them instantly, in multiple languages, and escalates the genuinely tricky cases to staff. It's the fastest way to cut helpdesk load and improve student satisfaction at once.

Why start here: low risk, high volume, immediate and visible impact.

2. Transparency & governance reporting

If your team spends weeks each quarter assembling council and accreditation reports, an agent that pulls from your source systems — with click-through provenance on every number — pays for itself fast. Done right, it makes you more accountable, not less. (See our transparency case study.)

Why start here: recurring, painful, and a strong signal that AI strengthens governance.

3. Admissions triage

During intake, staff are buried in document checks and eligibility screening. A triage agent handles the repetitive first pass against transparent criteria, replies to every applicant quickly, and hands officers clean, explained shortlists. (See our admissions case study.)

Why start here: seasonal pain, measurable speed-up, fairer outcomes.

4. Early-warning for student success

Attendance dips, missed submissions and falling grades are early signals of students at risk. An agent can surface these patterns and nudge mentors to reach out — turning data you already collect into timely, human intervention that improves retention.

Why start here: directly tied to outcomes and your mission.

5. Faculty & research copilots

Help faculty find funding calls, draft proposals, and summarise literature. Even modest time savings, multiplied across a department, free up real capacity for teaching and discovery.

Why start here: wins goodwill with faculty and supports your research agenda.

How to do it without the hype

  • Pick one use case and define what success looks like in numbers before you build.
  • Keep humans in the loop for any consequential decision.
  • Demand transparency: every agent action should be logged and explainable.
  • Keep your data yours — deploy in your environment, choose your model.
  • Start with a 4-week pilot, measure, then scale what works.
The institutions that win with AI won't be the ones that move fastest — they'll be the ones that move deliberately, transparently, and keep their people in charge.
