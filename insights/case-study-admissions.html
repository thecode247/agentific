Fairer, faster admissions with a triage agent

7 min read · Higher education · Illustrative engagement

The challenge. During peak intake, an admissions office received thousands of applications in a few weeks. Staff drowned in document checks and eligibility screening, response times stretched to weeks, and applicants from different counsellors sometimes got inconsistent answers. Good candidates dropped off simply because no one got back to them in time.

What we built

Agentific built an admissions triage agent that handles the repetitive first pass — so humans focus on judgement, not paperwork.

  • Document & eligibility checks. The agent verifies that each application is complete and meets published criteria, flagging exactly what's missing.
  • Consistent, transparent scoring. Every applicant is assessed against the same documented rubric, removing the luck of which desk you landed on.
  • Instant applicant updates. Candidates get an immediate, clear acknowledgement and a checklist of any next steps — in their language.
  • Bias checks built in. The agent's recommendations are monitored for disparate impact across groups, and protected attributes are excluded from scoring.

Humans stay in charge

The agent never makes the final admission decision. It prepares a clean, ranked, fully-explained shortlist; admissions officers review and decide. Every recommendation comes with the reasons behind it, so reviewers can challenge or override it — and that override is logged.

"It's not about replacing judgement. It's about giving our officers clean, fair shortlists instead of a mountain of PDFs." — Head of Admissions (illustrative)

The results

  • Time-to-first-response dropped from ~2 weeks to under an hour.
  • Incomplete applications fell sharply because applicants were told immediately what was missing.
  • More consistent decisions, with a documented, defensible rationale for each.
  • Staff reclaimed time for counselling and outreach — the work only humans can do.

Why it matters

Fairness and speed are usually framed as a trade-off. A well-designed triage agent delivers both: applicants get treated consistently and hear back quickly, while the institution gains a clear audit trail of how every decision was reached.
